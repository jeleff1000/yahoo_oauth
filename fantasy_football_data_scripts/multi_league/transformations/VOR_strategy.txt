Seasonal Evolution of Metrics

In-Season Updates: During the season, metrics should be updated weekly. Use running totals for VOR/PAR, ROI, etc. When evaluating a move mid-season (e.g. a week-8 pickup), only consider games and points up to that point or for the remainder of season, as appropriate. However, be cautious: early in-season replacement levels and averages are unstable, so include enough weeks (e.g. use 4+ games) before trusting these values.

Post-Season Stability: After the season ends, lock in metrics using full-season totals. Replacement levels become final (no more roster changes). All PAA, PAR, ROI, etc. use season totals or totals from acquisition to season end. Historical analysis should use these final values for consistency. At this point, one can also rank moves and trades definitively (e.g. â€œTop 5 draft steals of 2024â€ based on final DraftValueDelta).

Handling Late Changes: If players were traded late or picked up in playoffs, one might note a caveat or use a per-game metric because their raw totals are low. For legacy evaluation, it may be fair to compare their per-week impact (e.g. â€œaverage PAR per remaining weekâ€).
